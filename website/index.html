Music history: An Overview

Foreword

I will go through the main periods of music in this resource, to give you a better understanding of the evolution of music (and art) as humanity progresses.

Note: much of early classical music is comprised of religious music, which will be included in the list so that you get a better understanding of the era. If you do not believe in the faith, we hope that you are able to treat the religious music as pieces of art for you to enjoy and understand.

The Renaissance

During the Renaissance era, music became widespread as the invention of the printing press allowed for the wide circulation of music. Furthermore, the people in the Renaissance era believed in the concept of the "universal man"

The main stimulus for music-making in this era was text. Music was mainly used to enhance the meaning and value of texts. Therefore, there was a greater change in mood as a piece progresses. Generally, its tone sounds more relaxed and stable, and each vocal line in choir arrangements would have great independence over one another, and phrases overlapped to create a seamless flow of sound.

Generally, the instrumentation was for solo voice with instrument, but occasionally there would be instrumental music (usually for dancing) and choral arrangements too.

Listening list:

  • Flow my tears, a renaissance lute song

  • Passamezo and Galliard, an instrumental dance

  • As Vesta was Descending, a madrigal piece about Vesta (the goddess of the hearth) from Greek mythology. Look out for the wildly independent individual vocal lines in this piece.

  • Ave Maria, Virgo Serena, a renaissance motet (a small work for choir)
